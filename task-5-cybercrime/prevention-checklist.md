# Stay Safe Online: A Guide for Students

As your Digital Ambassador, I’ve compiled these 8 essential tips to help you navigate the web securely. Digital literacy is our best defense against cyber threats.

### 🛡️ General Cyber Hygiene
1. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Secure your college email, LinkedIn, and social media accounts with an extra layer of security beyond just a password.
2. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using your birthdate or "123456." Use a mix of symbols, numbers, and capital letters for every platform.
3. **Verify Links Before Clicking:** Hover over links in emails or messages to see the actual URL. If it looks "gibberish" or misspelled (e.g., "vltbhopal" instead of "vitbhopal"), do not click.
4. **Update Software Regularly:** Keep your OS and mobile apps updated to ensure you have the latest security patches against malware.
5. **Audit App Permissions:** Check your phone settings and revoke camera, microphone, or location access for apps that don't strictly need them.
6. **Public Wi-Fi Caution:** Avoid logging into bank accounts or entering sensitive data while using free public Wi-Fi at cafes or transit hubs. Use a VPN if necessary.

### 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Critical)
7. **The "Receive Money" Rule:** Always remember that you **NEVER** need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to enter a PIN to "authorize a credit," it is a scam.
8. **Verify the Payee Name:** Before hitting 'Send' on any UPI app, double-check the name that appears after scanning a QR code or entering a VPA to ensure it matches the intended recipient.

### 🚨 How to Report a Crime
If you or a friend are targeted by cybercriminals, immediate reporting is vital:
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) 
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Available 24x7)
