# Task 4: Professional Communication & Etiquette

### Overview
This module focused on the transition from casual social messaging to professional academic and corporate communication. As a Student Digital Ambassador, mastering these nuances is essential for effective outreach and representation.

### Folder Contents
1. **Professional_Emails.pdf:** Contains two formal drafts—an assignment extension request to a professor and an internship inquiry to a recruiter.
2. **social-media-checklist.md:** A 10-point guide on responsible digital footprints for college students.

### Reflection: The Impact of Poor Communication
A common scenario where poor digital communication causes issues is during high-stakes group projects. I have observed instances where a student uses overly casual language or "text-speak" in a formal Discord or WhatsApp group with a faculty coordinator. This often leads to a "tone-deaf" message that can be perceived as disrespectful, causing delays in approvals or a breakdown in professional trust.

What could have been done differently is the application of "Email Etiquette" rules to instant messaging: using a clear greeting, stating the context immediately, and maintaining a respectful tone regardless of the platform. This experience taught me that digital literacy isn't just about what you say, but how the recipient perceives your professional intent.
